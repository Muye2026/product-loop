# Structure Spec

## Mounting or placement logic

- Use a broad silicone anti-slip base with an internal weighted plate.
- Avoid permanent visible adhesive on the top or side surfaces.
- Keep the center of mass low and close to the dashboard contact plane.

## Target envelope

- Main body target: approximately 125 x 42 x 14 mm.
- Number window target: approximately 92 x 18 mm.
- Slider cover target: approximately 105 x 22 x 3 mm.
- Base/pad target: approximately 120 x 36 x 5 mm.

## Internal zoning

- Front center: number display tray and visible digit window.
- Front/top layer: sliding privacy cover and guide track.
- Lower layer: captured silicone pad and ballast plate.
- Ends: slider stops and anti-rattle details.

## Component assumptions

- Segmented printed digit cards are the selected baseline.
- Sliding privacy cover is the selected baseline.
- Silicone anti-slip pad plus internal weighted plate is the selected baseline.
- Fallback display envelope is 98 x 22 x 4 mm if card supplier changes.

## Packaging constraints

- The industrial design must preserve a shallow straight track.
- The slider tab needs finger access without becoming a decorative bump.
- The bottom contact footprint must remain broad enough for dashboard stability.
- Material choices must consider sunlight, heat, abrasion, and pad aging.

## Serviceability

- Digit cards should be replaceable without opening the whole shell.
- Slider should be removable or testable during prototype assembly.
- Pad attachment should be inspectable before final bonding or capture design.

## Structural risks

- Track tolerance can create rattle or binding.
- A thin cover may warp under cabin heat.
- Pad creep can reduce stability.
- Premium metal shells may increase heat soak near the windshield.

## Manufacturing boundary

- This spec does not certify cabin-heat durability.
- Exact supplier dimensions and material grades must be verified before detailed CAD.
- Electronics are intentionally excluded unless illumination becomes a hard requirement.
