# Concept Directions

## Direction A: Sliding Rail

- Keywords: slim, mechanical, precise, calm, dashboard-safe
- Summary: A low horizontal body with a visible but restrained sliding privacy cover. The number window sits behind a precise front rail, and the lower base reads as a stable soft-contact surface.
- Component basis: segmented printed digit cards, sliding cover, silicone anti-slip base, internal ballast.
- Structural plausibility: strong; the component stack supports a thin product if the track is controlled.
- Main risks: slider rattle, dust in the track, and heat aging of the bottom pad.
- Validation before CAD: confirm digit card thickness, slider friction, and pad durometer.

## Direction B: Lift Shield

- Keywords: protective, premium, hinged, tactile, compact
- Summary: A small hinged cover flips up or down to reveal the number. The product reads more like a protective instrument than a flat label.
- Component basis: printed digit cards, flip cover, hinge pins, silicone or weighted base.
- Structural plausibility: medium; hinge geometry increases side thickness and needs fatigue review.
- Main risks: hinge durability, dashboard clearance, and visual bulk.
- Validation before CAD: confirm hinge barrel size and opening angle.

## Direction C: Light Strip Reserve

- Keywords: readable, night-ready, layered, serviceable, thicker
- Summary: A slightly thicker body reserves room for optional reflective or low-power illuminated visibility while preserving the same privacy slider logic.
- Component basis: digit cards or translucent film, optional light guide, serviceable electronics zone.
- Structural plausibility: medium; useful only if night visibility becomes a hard requirement.
- Main risks: battery safety, service access, heat, and material yellowing.
- Validation before CAD: confirm whether active illumination is required.
