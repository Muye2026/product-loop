# Appearance Spec

## Intent

Create a compact monitor-top device that reads as a mature desktop tool with a mild human touch. Keep the product quiet, stable, and easy to parse at a glance.

## Primary silhouette

- Long horizontal bar with restrained height.
- Visual mass concentrated near the main front body, with support logic expressed behind it.
- End treatment should feel balanced and controlled rather than decorative.
- Preserve near-symmetry in the outer shell while allowing internal functions to remain asymmetric.

## Front-face hierarchy

- Primary focal point: central sensing zone.
- Secondary information area: left display window.
- Tertiary area: right service/control zone.
- Keep front-facing features aligned on a clear horizontal logic, not a floating composition.

## Side and top read

- Keep thickness compact and consistent.
- Use small chamfers or facets rather than heavy rounding.
- Ensure the side view makes the product look supported, not magically suspended.

## Feature language

- Display window should read as a precise information slot.
- Sensor island should look intentional and functional, not ornamental.
- Service ports and button cues should be understated but discoverable.
- Seams should support a plausible shell split and service strategy.

## CMF guidance

- Main shell: muted technical finish with low visual noise.
- Functional windows: darker contrast panels.
- Accent use: minimal and localized.
- Avoid glossy toy-like color blocking.

## Forbidden moves

- oversized central black band
- decorative vents with no function
- exaggerated asymmetry on the front face
- playful facial expressions or mascot cues
