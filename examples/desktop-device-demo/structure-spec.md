# Structure Spec

## Mounting or placement logic

- Use a monitor-top support strategy with a small forward contact point and a rear support arm.
- Avoid spring-loaded clamp complexity in the first concept pass.
- Rely on stable contact geometry and center-of-mass control.

## Target envelope

- Keep the main body within approximately 140 x 38 x 20 mm.
- Treat rear support depth as a controlled extension rather than a separate large assembly.
- Preserve basic compatibility with common 24-27 inch monitor thickness ranges.

## Internal zoning

- Front-left: display keep-out.
- Front-center: vision sensor zone.
- Front-right: service and control area.
- Rear/lower volume: support hardware and sound output volume.

## Component assumptions

- small display module with direct front read
- compact camera package with centered alignment requirement
- main board occupying most of the right-half internal length
- buzzer or micro-speaker placed away from the primary front focal area
- one service-accessible button near the right side

## Serviceability

- Preserve direct access to the service port area.
- Avoid any shell split that forces full disassembly for routine cable access.
- Keep the camera alignment zone mechanically protected.

## Structural risks

- right-side service window may become too crowded
- rear support arm may look thinner than its real structural demand
- camera alignment may conflict with shallow depth targets
- support and shell split may compete for the same internal volume

## Manufacturing boundary

- This phase does not define wall thickness, snap details, screws, draft, or tolerance stack.
- Detailed board fit, cable routing, and pad material still require downstream validation.
