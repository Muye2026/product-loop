# Concept Directions

## Direction A: Quiet Bar

- Keywords: slim, stable, restrained, monitor-native, precise
- Summary: A long, compact bar that reads like a mature monitor accessory. The front face is calm, with a left display, a center sensing zone, and a small right-side service/control zone.
- Why it fits: Best fit for low visual noise and everyday desk use.
- Structural plausibility: Strong; easy to map into a front body plus rear support logic.
- Risks: Can lose personality if the front hierarchy becomes too flat.
- Validation before CAD: confirm board keep-out and port access inside the right-side zone.

## Direction B: Lens Core

- Keywords: focused, central, confident, sensor-first, compact
- Summary: A more concentrated device where the sensor island takes visual priority and the side zones support it. The result feels more like a smart device and less like a simple accessory.
- Why it fits: Useful when the sensing function must read as the product core.
- Structural plausibility: Medium; center thickness and balance need care.
- Risks: Can become visually heavy and overemphasize the camera.
- Validation before CAD: confirm balance and support-arm leverage from the side.

## Direction C: Soft Facet

- Keywords: warm, geometric, approachable, tidy, light
- Summary: A restrained faceted shell that softens the device without making it look playful. Friendly, but still credible as a functional desktop tool.
- Why it fits: Best balance between usability and warmth.
- Structural plausibility: Medium to strong; depends on keeping the support and service zones honest.
- Risks: Friendly shaping may conflict with internal service-space needs.
- Validation before CAD: confirm that the softened end geometry does not steal too much internal width.

## Selected Direction

Choose `Direction A: Quiet Bar` as the main path.

Reason:

- strongest match to the brief
- easiest to preserve mounting credibility
- cleanest bridge from render work into a structured design pack
