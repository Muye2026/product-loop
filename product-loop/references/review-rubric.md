# Review Rubric

Use this rubric for concept review, spec review, and CAD iteration review.

## Score Bands

- `Pass`: good enough to keep moving
- `Partial`: direction is viable, but at least one blocking issue remains
- `Fail`: do not continue without rework

## Review Categories

### 1. Brief fit

- Does the direction satisfy the primary product goal?
- Does it match the main use scenario?
- Does it preserve the required functions?

### 2. Visual coherence

- Is the silhouette consistent from front, side, and top?
- Are focal and secondary features ordered clearly?
- Does the design feel like one product rather than assembled fragments?

### 3. Structure plausibility

- Can the proposed mounting or placement logic actually work?
- Do component envelopes fit the claimed form factor?
- Are service areas reachable?

### 4. Component selection credibility

- Were comparable products or application cases inspected when research access was available?
- Are the selected or assumed components plausible for the product goal?
- Are size, interface, mounting, power, material, or sourcing assumptions explicit?

### 5. Packaging feasibility

- Do selected components fit the claimed envelope and visual direction?
- Are display windows, mechanisms, magnets, pads, ports, batteries, or service areas accounted for?
- Are heat, sunlight, vibration, abrasion, sealing, or durability risks called out when relevant?

### 6. Translation quality

- Do the appearance and structure specs describe actionable constraints?
- Does the design pack capture the geometry-driving rules?
- Are assumptions and unknowns explicit?

### 7. CAD readiness

- Can a downstream modeler begin with the provided artifacts?
- Are critical dimensions and keep-outs defined?
- Are the highest-risk items called out clearly?

## Review Output

For each category, capture:

- status
- evidence
- blocking issue, if any
- next action

Use short, operational statements. Avoid generic praise.
